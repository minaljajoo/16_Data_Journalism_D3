Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Research has shown a link between poverty and poor health. People with more income tends to be healthier and live longer. What explains the connection? Many factors. For one, lack of care. We know that 18.5% of the U.S. non-elderly population does not have health insurance, with low-income families especially vulnerable to being uninsured. Nearly 70% of the uninsured population is poor or near-poor. The uninsured tend to forego preventative care and to wait until an illness is severe before seeking medical care.

In the U.S., people living below the poverty level have higher rates of cigarette smoking than the general population.

The U.S. is one of the wealthiest countries in the world and accordingly has high obesity rates.

Mississippi is one of the low-income state with higher obesity rate (35.5%) on the other hand Washington D.C. is one of the high-income state with lower obesity rate (21.7%).
